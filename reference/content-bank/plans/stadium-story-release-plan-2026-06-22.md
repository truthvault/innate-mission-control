# Stadium story release plan

Date saved: 2026-06-22
Owner: Content
Status: planning, approval-gated

## Working story

A Christchurch-made, West Coast beech hospitality piece, brought into One NZ Stadium through Aitkens and Venues Ōtautahi, as part of a wider local procurement and local hospitality story.

Core angle:

> One NZ Stadium is not only showcasing local food. Some of the objects carrying that food were also made locally, from New Zealand timber, by a Christchurch workshop.

## Current priority

Do not approach publications yet.

The next practical work is:

1. Get Richard's comfort around the testimonial and Aitkens side of the story.
2. Create the first polished draft for Richard Page and Daniel Shanks to review.
3. Include a simple outline of how Innate intends to use and share the story.
4. Make clear that partner-specific versions can be drafted for Aitkens and Venues Ōtautahi so the story fits their own audience and objectives.
5. Only after the primary approvals and timing are clear, decide which external publications to approach and in what order.

## Approval gates

Need explicit approval before any public release for:

- using Venues Ōtautahi name;
- using Daniel Shanks' quote;
- using Aitkens name;
- using Charlotte's name or former role;
- saying Aitkens brought Innate into the project;
- using professional stadium photos;
- naming One NZ Stadium in a way that could imply endorsement;
- saying West Coast beech if that is the confirmed material for the specific pieces shown;
- pitching the story to external publications.

Proposed wording:

> If everyone is comfortable, we would like to use the story in a few staged ways: first as an Innate project story on our website, then as a short newsletter and social media post for our own audience. We can also draft a version specifically for Aitkens and a version for Venues Ōtautahi if that is useful, so the story fits each organisation properly rather than just copying our own wording. Once those pieces and the timing are agreed, we may look at whether the story is suitable for a small number of local or industry publications, but we would check the right approach before doing that.

   - Angle: local collaboration, West Coast beech, stadium hospitality, workshop judgement.

2. Innate newsletter
   - Warm audience and high trust value.
   - Angle: the small timber detail inside One NZ Stadium.

3. Instagram and Facebook carousel
   - Strong visual release if photos are good.
   - Angle: from Christchurch workshop to stadium hospitality.

4. LinkedIn
   - Use only if the post has a business reason to exist.

   - Avoid brochure voice and avoid making it a generic furniture sales post.

## External publication approach

Do not pitch external publications until approval and timing are clear.

Reason:

- Some publications may want exclusivity or at least a fresh angle.
- A story that has already been sitting publicly on Innate's website for a month may be less attractive.
- The best sequence may be partner approval first, then selected publication pitch, then Innate owned-channel release, or a coordinated release.

Potential publication lanes to evaluate later:

1. ChristchurchNZ / Christchurch Economic Ambition
   - Angle: small concrete example of local procurement, regenerative economy, local manufacturing, and local spend.

2. Avenues Magazine
   - Angle: the quiet local details inside the city’s new stadium.

3. Canterbury Today
   - Angle: Christchurch business contributes to new stadium visitor experience.

4. Business Canterbury
   - Angle: local manufacturing meeting major event infrastructure.

5. Business South / Waterford Press
   - Angle: small South Island manufacturer in a flagship regional project.

6. The Press / Stuff local
   - Angle: beyond food vendors, local businesses shaping the stadium experience.

7. Chris Lynch Media
   - Angle: Christchurch-made details inside the stadium.

8. Hospitality Business NZ or Restaurant & Cafe
   - Angle: local procurement and custom serviceware in a major hospitality venue.

9. Architecture / design media
   - Lower priority unless imagery and design detail are strong.
   - Angle: material detail and local craft inside a major public venue.

## Best sequencing options

### Option A: Owned-channel first

1. Approvals from Richard, Daniel, and any required organisational contacts.
2. Innate website story.
3. Newsletter.
4. Social posts.
5. Partner versions.
6. Later publication pitches.

Risk:

- External media may see it as already used.

### Option B: Publication-first

1. Approvals from Richard, Daniel, and any required organisational contacts.
2. Prepare approved story package and images.
3. Pitch one or two best-fit publications first.
4. If no interest after a defined period, release through Innate channels.
5. If accepted, coordinate timing and then publish own-channel follow-up.

Benefit:

- Better chance with Avenues, Canterbury Today, Business Canterbury, or ChristchurchNZ.

### Option C: Partner-first

1. Approvals from Richard, Daniel, and any required organisational contacts.
2. Prepare Aitkens and Venues Ōtautahi versions.
3. Let one partner publish or co-publish first.
4. Innate follows with behind-the-work version.
5. Pitch external publications with the partner proof if still fresh.

Benefit:

- Relationship-safe and may give the story more credibility.

## Notes for future drafting

- Keep the story about the work and the local supply chain, not a sales pitch.
- Avoid overclaiming sustainability.
- Confirm the exact timber species before publishing.
- Avoid suggesting Venues Ōtautahi or One NZ Stadium formally endorses Innate unless they approve that implication.
- No em dash characters in new copy.

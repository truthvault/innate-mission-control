# Content correction log

## 2026-06-22 - Stadium story opening sounded salesy

Guido rejected the opening phrase: “The scale. The roof. The seating. The sense/feeling of Christchurch stepping into a new chapter.”

Failure mode:
- Too grand and civic.
- Sounds like copy trying to be more important than the actual Innate role.
- Reads salesy rather than authentic, relatable and simple.

Durable rule:
- For Innate project stories, do not open with grand architectural/civic framing unless Guido explicitly asks for that register.
- Start with the actual Innate detail: timber, object, brief, workshop, supplier relationship, or what people use.
- Let the wider meaning emerge from the facts instead of announcing it.

Applied fix:
- Replaced the Stadium draft opening with a simpler object-led version starting from the serving board under the food.
- Patched the Innate authentic marketing skill with this rule.

Follow-up correction:
- Guido rejected the replacement because “most people will never stop to think” makes the audience feel incapable or talked down to.
- The issue is not only grand language. It is also reader dignity.

Durable rule:
- Never frame an Innate insight around what “most people” fail to notice, understand or think about.
- Make the reader feel special, capable and invited into the detail.
- Do not make Innate or the writer the clever observer above the audience.

Applied fix:
- Paused the Stadium draft opening instead of generating another variant.
- Created `innate-writing-sop-humble-helpful-voice-2026-06-22.md`.
- Patched the Innate authentic marketing skill to require the reader-respect gate before project stories.

Follow-up correction:
- Guido rejected the next calibration because it still treated object, material and practical brief as the main candidates, while the strongest angle was the partner relationship and collaboration.
- The miss was generic content thinking: reducing a serious partner-led commercial project to an object/material story.

Durable rule:
- For commercial, civic, procurement or collaboration stories, identify the central relationship before choosing an opening.
- Default hierarchy: partnership/collaboration, shared brief or trust, each partner's role, then object/material/workshop response.
- Do not reduce a relationship-led project to a product caption.

Applied fix:
- Patched the writing SOP to replace “start with the thing” with “start with the strongest relationship.”
- Patched the Innate authentic marketing skill with a partner-first gate for project stories.

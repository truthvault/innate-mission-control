# Draft story

## A local partnership inside One NZ Stadium

This story begins with the relationship that brought local timber work into a major hospitality project.

Venues Ōtautahi wanted the stadium hospitality experience to carry more than scale and service. Daniel Shanks' note makes that clear. The brief was about local producers, local suppliers, sustainable choices, regional food, and products that feel connected to Aotearoa.

Aitkens helped turn that intent into practical supply. They understood the hospitality setting, the service requirements, and the kind of local maker that could fit the brief.

That is where Innate's role sits: one Christchurch workshop, brought into a larger hospitality project through a partner relationship, making timber pieces that support the food, the venue and the local procurement story.

## What the collaboration made possible

For Daniel Shanks, General Manager / Executive Chef for Culinary Services at Venues Ōtautahi, the relationship between local hospitality and local making is central to the project.

> "Working with Innate has been a natural fit for us. As we continue to shape the hospitality experience at One NZ Stadium, it makes total sense to work with local producers, craftsmen and suppliers whose values align with our own.
>
> "The handcrafted West Coast beech brings an authentic sense of place to our hospitality offering. They're beautifully crafted, tell a genuine local story, and provide a platform to showcase the incredible food and produce of our region.
>
> "We're proud to work with a Christchurch business that is committed to craftsmanship, sustainability and creating products that reflect the identity of Aotearoa.
>
> "Partnerships like this help create a more meaningful and authentic guest experience while supporting our local economy and community."
>
> Daniel Shanks, General Manager / Executive Chef for Culinary Services, Venues Ōtautahi

That quote sets the logic for the story.

The timber pieces are not separate from the hospitality experience. They are part of how the food is presented, how regional produce is framed, and how the venue expresses its local procurement values in something guests and staff can actually use.

## Aitkens, Venues Ōtautahi and a local workshop

Aitkens has been supplying New Zealand's hospitality industry since the 1950s. For a project like this, their role is broader than finding items in a catalogue. It is understanding the venue, the service environment, the scale of the order, and which suppliers can meet the brief without losing the intent behind it.

Through that relationship, Innate was asked to produce a set of timber hospitality pieces for One NZ Stadium.

The final supply included large serving platters, smaller platters, platters with drip grooves, large cutlery holders, and small and large napkin dispensers with black powder coated metal arms.

They needed to be robust, repeatable and practical for staff. They also needed to sit comfortably in a venue where local food, regional suppliers, sustainability and the identity of Aotearoa are part of the guest experience.

## Local materials, made for daily service

All of the timber pieces were made in Christchurch from West Coast beech and finished with a clear foodsafe natural oil.

Those details matter because they are part of the same decision-making Daniel describes: local producers, aligned values, sustainability, craftsmanship, and a genuine local story.

West Coast beech is not a generic material choice. It is New Zealand native timber with its own provenance, variation and responsibility. Used well, it gives the pieces warmth and a clear connection to place without needing to be loud.

The natural oil finish matters for the same reason. It keeps the timber honest, supports a more natural material story, and suits pieces designed to be handled, cleaned and used in a working hospitality environment.

In the workshop, the job was not making one hero piece for one room. It was stacks of boards, repeated components, consistent dimensions, finishing, packing and delivery. The work was in making many pieces feel like one family while still letting the timber keep its natural character.

## A platform for regional food

Daniel's line about providing "a platform to showcase the incredible food and produce of our region" is the centre of the story.

The serving pieces are not the food. They are not the main event.

But they help hold the food in the right context.

A board made from West Coast beech in a Christchurch workshop carries a different feeling from a generic imported service item. It supports the food story, the supplier story, and the wider intention behind the stadium's hospitality programme.

That is the impact of the collaboration. Venues Ōtautahi set the hospitality direction. Aitkens connected the right supply relationship. Innate made the timber pieces locally, using New Zealand native timber and natural oils in a way that fitted the brief.

## Made here, with others

One NZ Stadium is a major public venue, but this story works because it is specific.

A hospitality team wanted local values to show up in the guest experience. Aitkens helped connect that intent with practical supply. Innate made the timber pieces here in Christchurch from West Coast beech.

The result is a set of serving boards, cutlery holders and napkin dispensers with a role inside the wider hospitality story.

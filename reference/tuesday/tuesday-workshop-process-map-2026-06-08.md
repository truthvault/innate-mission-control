## Standard External Lead Times

- Steel fabrication and powder coating: 2 weeks normal, 3 weeks safer after purchase order/spec confirmation.
- Westimber laminated timber panels: about 2 weeks after purchase order/spec confirmation.
- Timber in stock is still pulled and strapped for Westimber collection/laminating when a laminated table top is required.
- Precision Woodworks CNC shape cutting: add about 2 weeks after Westimber for shapes other than rectangle, square, round, or pill.
- Danish oval, classic oval, pebble, organic, and other custom shapes need the Precision Woodworks CNC gate.
- Precision Woodworks jobs require booking Pinpoint to collect the CNC-cut panel(s) and return them to the workshop.
- If a job depends on both steel and a laminated panel, use the later material-ready date.
- Supplier lead times are planning gates. Sanding/coating should not be suggested before the relevant material/top/base is expected to be ready.

## Finish Timing

- Allow at least one working day between coats; two working days is better when the schedule allows.
- Coating tasks should respect Dylan's workshop days: Monday to Thursday.
- Blackwash is 2 stain coats plus 2 clear coats.
- Clear is 3 clear coats.
- Allow at least 2 working days after final coat before QC/assembly/packing; a full week or more is better when the schedule allows.
- QC, photos, assembly, wrapping, and freight/collection planning should happen after final coat curing, not immediately after the last coat.

## Dining Table Baseline

1. Order Loaded: Guido checks invoice/spec/payment/customer promise/supplier needs/delivery method before workshop trust.
2. POs sent / supplier requirements confirmed.
3. Timber pulled and strapped for Westimber collection/laminating where required.
4. Materials received, including laminated top/timber, steel base/hardware, and ordered materials.
5. Precision Woodworks CNC, only for non-standard shapes such as Danish oval, classic oval, pebble, organic, or other custom shapes.
6. Book Pinpoint return from Precision Woodworks, only when CNC is required.
7. Cut / machine / prep only when the shape or construction requires in-workshop cutting, usually round tops.
8. Stress cuts just before sanding and coating.
9. Clear finish: sand and first clear coat.
10. Clear finish: second coat.
11. Clear finish: third/final clear coat.
12. Blackwash/colourwash finish instead uses: sand and first stain coat; second stain coat; first clear coat; final clear coat.
13. Cure.
14. QC + photos.
15. Assemble for local delivery, or box/prepare for freight.
16. Pack / wrap.
17. Balance invoice / payment release check.
18. Guido books freight or confirms collection/delivery for now.
19. Customer update.

Standard rectangle, square, round, and pill dining tables do not need the Precision Woodworks CNC gate. Standard rectangular dining tables generally do not need a separate cut/machine/prep task after materials are ready.

## Trust Rule

Suggested plans are allowed to be conservative. A slightly later but believable plan is better than a neat plan that ignores supplier waits, drying time, or Nick/Dylan availability.

# Innate August America Mode Operating Plan

Date: 2026-05-19
Owner: Hermes/default for Innate business operations
Deadline: August 2026, when Guido is in America for about one month

## Goal

By August, Guido should be able to run Innate while overseas with internet most days and about 1–2 hours/day available.

The target is not full autonomy. The target is exception-based control:

> Guido makes high-value approvals and decisions. Hermes, Tuesday, Xero, Gmail, Monday, Shopify, and monitoring systems reconstruct reality for him.

## Operating principle

Build **America Mode**:

- One daily command post.
- One approval queue.
- Clear escalation rules.
- Draft-first, no surprise outward actions.
- Cashflow and customer promises first.
- Systems improve weekly from real friction, not abstract automation.

## What Guido should experience in August

Daily rhythm:

1. Read one Innate Daily Control message.
2. Approve/decline any drafted actions.
3. Answer at most one or two strategic questions.
4. Close laptop unless there is a true fire.

The daily message should say either:

- "No action needed from you today", or
- "These are the 1–3 things that need your decision."

## Approval boundaries

Hermes may automatically:

- read Gmail, Xero, Tuesday/Supabase, Monday, Shopify reports, logs, and relevant internal files;
- draft emails/SMS/content/internal notes;
- classify leads and risks;
- prepare proposed status updates;
- create internal reports and recommendations;
- run read-only checks and monitoring;
- flag exceptions.

Hermes must not automatically:

- send emails/SMS/customer messages;
- publish website/content/product changes;
- pay bills or move money;
- create/send/void Xero invoices or bills;
- update live Monday/Tues/Supabase/Shopify records if customer/team-visible;
- delete records/files;
- change provider/gateway/cron settings;
- make commitments to customers or suppliers.

## Three operating lanes

### Green lane: automatic internal work

Read-only inspection, drafting, summarising, risk detection, and internal notes.

Examples:

- Find lead emails needing reply.
- Draft replies for approval.
- Summarise Xero bills due 7/14/30 days.
- Flag production jobs at risk.
- Draft content ideas.
- Monitor website/enquiry issues.

### Approval lane: Guido yes/no

Anything outward-facing, financial, live-system-changing, or relationship-sensitive.

Examples:

- Send this follow-up email?
- Send this SMS?
- Update this customer status?
- Chase this invoice?
- Publish this post/page?
- Remove/cancel this phone number?
- Pay/delay this bill?

### Emergency lane: interrupt immediately

Only for real fires:

- cash/payment risk above threshold;
- customer-visible crisis;
- production blocker costing days;
- delivery promise at risk;
- legal/accounting/security issue;
- website/enquiry flow broken;
- VIP or relationship-sensitive customer moment.

## Five workstreams

### 1. Leads and email

August outcome:

- Tuesday `/leads` is the sales control surface.
- Hermes checks Gmail + Tuesday/Supabase + known lead context.
- Hot cash leads are prioritised.
- Hermes drafts replies and follow-ups.
- Guido approves sends or changes.

Minimum working version:

- Daily list: hot leads, stale leads, waiting-on-Guido leads, waiting-on-customer leads.
- Drafted replies for the top 1–3 leads.
- Clear "send / edit / park" approval options.

Needed systems:

- Gmail search/triage workflow.
- Supabase/Tues lead overview.
- Lead status buckets.
- Draft-only email generation with verified signature.
- Eventually 2talk SMS draft/approval path.

### 2. Production and Nick

August outcome:

- Production Plan is simple enough for Nick.
- Hermes flags work falling behind before Guido has to ask.
- Customer promise risk is visible.
- Nick feedback loops improve the board without making it feel like a new system.

Minimum working version:

- `/production/plan` shows jobs in a plain workshop-board view.
- Flags: overdue, no due date, material delay, delivery risk, customer update needed.
- Hermes drafts one-line customer update when production risk appears.

Needed systems:

- Monday/read-only production source or Tuesday mirror.
- Nick-friendly board language.
- Daily production exception scan.
- Weekly "what confused Nick?" improvement loop.

### 3. Cashflow and Xero

August outcome:

- Xero remains source of truth for bills/payables and receivables.
- Hermes gives a cash cockpit and exception report.
- Guido approves money decisions, not reconstructs them.

Minimum working version:

- Bills due next 7/14/30 days.
- Expected payment dates.
- Overdue invoices and promised receipts.
- Supplier pressure / account emails needing reply.
- Red/yellow/green cash risk.

Needed systems:

- Read-only Xero summary.
- Accounts Gmail scan.
- Optional bank/cash snapshot if safe and available.
- Approval queue: pay / delay / chase / ask accountant.

### 4. Website, configurator, content

August outcome:

- Website explains enough that leads arrive with useful info.
- Configurator/enquiry activity is visible in Mission Control.
- Hermes drafts content and catches breakage.

Minimum working version:

- Key product/collection pages have clear copy, CTAs, lead time/freight expectations.
- Enquiry/configurator submissions reliably log to Mission Control/Tues.
- Monitoring flags broken links, missing images, noindex, bad enquiry flow, major SEO issues.
- Draft-only content cadence: workshop note, material story, project story, buyer guide.

Needed systems:

- Shopify/product-page monitoring.
- Configurator event logging.
- Enquiry form proof/checks.
- Content draft templates.

### 5. Daily control and continuous improvement

August outcome:

- One daily digest and one approval queue run the business rhythm.
- Weekly improvement converts friction into automation.

Minimum working version:

Daily Innate Control message:

- Cash risk: green/yellow/red.
- Hot leads.
- Emails/SMS drafted for approval.
- Production blockers.
- Customer promises at risk.
- Website/configurator issues.
- One most important decision.
- Safe-to-ignore note if no action needed.

Weekly improvement review:

- What broke this week?
- What did Guido have to explain twice?
- What did Nick find confusing?
- What did customers ask repeatedly?
- What should become a template/script/check?

```text
Customer/production risk:
1. [order/customer] — [risk] — [recommended next action]

Approvals ready:
1. Send email to [name]? [yes/edit/no]
2. Update [system/status]? [yes/no]

- [only if important]

One decision needed:
- [single highest-leverage question]

If quiet:
No action needed from you today.
```

## Key rule

Do not automate chaos. Make reality visible, draft the next move, then ask for the smallest safe approval.
